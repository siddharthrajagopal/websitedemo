Objection Handling · When the Customer Says…

"We're standardising on Data 360 — we don't need another data platform."
"Data 360 is the activation layer. The question is what feeds it. Today you're feeding it ungoverned data from ERP and Supply Chain via point-to-point pipes. IDMC and MuleSoft are the governed fabric — so Data 360 is trusted by audit, finance, and Agentforce."
"We have Databricks / a lakehouse. Isn't that our data platform?"
"A lake stores bytes. It doesn't produce a golden customer record, a business glossary, or a stewardship workflow. That's Cloud MDM + CDGC. The lake gets better because IDMC and MuleSoft sit on top of it."
"Data 360 has Zero Copy to Databricks, Snowflake, BigQuery."
"Zero Copy is plumbing — it doesn't govern, master, or quality-check the data flowing through. CDGC gives you the policies, Cloud MDM gives you the single record, CDQ makes sure it's trustworthy. Zero Copy + IDMC and MuleSoft = enterprise-ready."
"Our Agentforce pilot is fine on Salesforce data."
"Pilots are fine. Production isn't. The first time Agentforce sends a 1-hour L2 response to a customer with a $250K/hr SLA penalty, your pilot becomes a board-level incident. IDMC and MuleSoft are how you go from pilot to trusted production."
"We only plan to use one agent — Agentforce. Why do we need Agent Fabric?"
"Today, one. In 18 months, ten — Service, Invoicing, Legal-drafting, SRE, Marketing, you name it. Agent Fabric is what stops them from disagreeing with each other — and IDMC and MuleSoft supply the shared metadata and APIs they all ground on. You buy it before the chaos arrives."
"We'll just integrate point-to-point when we need non-SFDC data."
"5 systems = 20 integrations. 10 systems = 90. That's the O(n²) tax — and every broken pipe is a wrong answer from Agentforce. IDMC CDI (bulk) + MuleSoft (real-time), governed by CDGC, give you one hub that scales with the business."
